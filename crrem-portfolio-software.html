What Software Can Generate CRREM Misalignment Years for a UK Property Portfolio?

Comparing the options for portfolio-level CRREM pathway analysis — from free single-asset tools to enterprise ESG data platforms — and what each approach actually delivers.

Several software platforms can now generate CRREM misalignment years for UK property portfolios, ranging from free browser-based tools for single-asset calculations to enterprise ESG data platforms costing £20,000–£100,000+ per year that embed CRREM methodology within broader sustainability management systems — the right choice depends on portfolio size, existing data infrastructure, and whether you need the stranding year alone or a full transition risk report.

The demand for CRREM-aligned software has accelerated sharply since the CRREM Foundation announced it would retire its Excel tool on 1 July 2026 and since INREV referenced CRREM as a material factor in property valuation methodology. Fund managers who previously relied on the free Excel tool now need an alternative, and investment committees are increasingly asking for portfolio-level stranding profiles as part of their climate risk governance.

What Matters at Portfolio Level

The single-asset CRREM calculation is straightforward: input property type, location, energy consumption by carrier, and floor area; the tool returns the misalignment year. At portfolio level, the challenge is different. You need aggregation across dozens or hundreds of assets with different property types, different energy profiles, and different data completeness — and you need the output in a format that investment committees and bank credit teams can act on.

The key features that distinguish portfolio-level CRREM software from single-asset tools are batch processing (importing a portfolio from a spreadsheet rather than entering one building at a time), aggregated risk profiles (what percentage of your portfolio is already stranded, at-risk within 5 years, or aligned), scenario analysis (what happens to the portfolio's stranding profile if you retrofit the 10 worst-performing assets), and reporting output (professional documents suitable for LP reporting, GRESB submissions, or board presentations).

The gap in the market: Enterprise ESG data platforms (Measurabl, Deepki, Scaler) do an excellent job of collecting and managing sustainability data, but they generally do not write investor-grade narrative reports.
